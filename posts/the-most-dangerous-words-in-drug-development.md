This is one of the most common root causes behind "unexpected" regulatory setbacks.

It usually shows up when teams are under pressure and decisions feel uncomfortable:

- Formulation isn't final → "we'll lock it later"
- Analytical method isn't robust → "we'll improve it in Phase II"
- CMC story isn't consistent → "we'll tighten it after the submission"

The issue is that regulators don't just assess data. They assess whether your development logic holds together.

And the moment tox and early clinical data are generated, "later" becomes expensive:

- Changes trigger comparability work
- Bridging packages appear
- Timelines slip quietly

Good programs don't avoid problems. They avoid deferring decisions that create fragile narratives.
