Process Performance Qualification (PPQ) is the stage of validation where you demonstrate that your manufacturing process, as designed and controlled, can consistently produce compliant product at intended scale under routine conditions. It sits at the heart of Stage 2 process validation, bridging the move from development and clinical supply into commercial production. PPQ readiness is therefore not just about having a protocol; it is about having a process that is genuinely ready to be tested. 

A process is PPQ-ready when key elements are in place and proven: defined critical process parameters (CPPs) and critical quality attributes (CQAs), a practical and justified control strategy, qualified equipment and facilities, trained personnel, and robust analytics. Just as important is a solid process history, showing that the process performs reliably across prior development and clinical lots, and that variability is understood rather than simply observed.

Programs often discover PPQ gaps late, in areas such as:

* CPPs and ranges that were never truly challenged or justified at earlier scales, making PPQ more of an experiment than a confirmation.
* Incomplete process history tables, unclear linkage between development data and the intended commercial conditions, or missing rationales for changes.
* Documentation, sampling plans, and deviation handling that are not mature enough for a high-stakes, inspected PPQ campaign.

Treating PPQ readiness as a deliberate milestone changes how teams prepare:

* Use late-stage development to refine CPPs, CQAs, and in-process controls based on real data, not just initial assumptions.
* Build and maintain process history tables that clearly show how the process has evolved and how current conditions were chosen.
* Develop PPQ protocols that integrate process, analytics, sampling, acceptance criteria, and deviation management into a coherent, auditable plan.
